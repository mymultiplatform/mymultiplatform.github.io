What this division is for

The first job of the ad division is not “brand awareness.” It is to turn the strongest MYM offers into repeatable lead flow with simple routing and visible numbers.

  • Put each service behind one clear promise and one clear next action.
  • Use high-intent channels first before broad awareness spending.
  • Route all early leads into one inbox so nothing gets dropped.
  • Scale only after one lane shows qualified demand, not just clicks.

Offer Map

These are the three service lanes closest to real demand right now. Each lane needs its own ad promise, landing page, and response workflow.

Architectural / millwork packages

Target general contractors, developers, homeowners, and owners who need shop drawings, fabrication coordination, finish packages, or install-ready planning.

Lane 2

Software / AI operations tools

Target founders, operators, and teams that want practical automation, internal tools, or attention-control software with a clear business use case.

  • Landing page: /software/
Lane 3

Target warehouses, stores, worksites, and property operators that need guard coverage, fast deployment, or cleaner security presence.

  • Primary promise: dependable coverage with quick start.

  • Landing page: /mym.security/

Channel Mix

This channel choice is an inference based on current MYM services and official platform capabilities. The order favors channels that capture intent before channels that interrupt.

Priority 1

Google Search + local presence

Best for architecture / millwork and security because the lead already has intent. Search campaigns catch active demand, while a compliant Google Business Profile supports local trust for businesses with a location or service area.

  • Use separate campaigns by service lane and city/region.
  • Add strong assets and callouts so ads take more real estate.
  • Use lead forms only when the account meets Google’s eligibility requirements.
Priority 2

LinkedIn lead generation for B2B

Best fit for software / AI services and selected contractor outreach because the platform’s lead generation objective opens a native pre-filled form and keeps friction low.

  • Use one offer per ad set: audit, pilot, or consultation.
  • Keep forms short so completion stays high.
  • Focus on founders, operations leaders, and project decision-makers.
Priority 3

Landing pages + follow-up discipline

Ads do not rescue weak offers. Each service lane should point to one page, one CTA, one inbox, and one fast response workflow.

  • Every ad points to a single service page, not the whole homepage.
  • Use one subject line per lane in the inbox to classify leads quickly.
  • Reply fast, because response time is part of conversion rate.
Official platform notes behind this plan: Google documents Search campaigns, assets, Business Profile location/service-area eligibility, and lead form requirements; LinkedIn documents the lead generation objective, native Lead Gen Forms, and the recommendation to keep form fields light. The budget split and launch order here are MYM recommendations inferred from those platform capabilities.

Budget Shape

These are planning ranges, not platform quotes. They are designed to keep early spend controlled while proving which lane can actually produce qualified leads.

Test $1.2k / month

Use when the goal is learning fast with low burn. Suggested split: 70% Google Search for architecture / security, 20% LinkedIn for software outreach, 10% creative and landing-page tests.

Operating $2.4k / month

Use when one lane is already showing signals. Suggested split: 55% Search, 25% LinkedIn, 20% retests, new creatives, and follow-up support assets.

Scale $4.5k+ / month

Use only after booked calls or quote requests prove real pipeline. At this point, duplicate the winning lane into tighter geographies, job titles, or offer variations.

30-Day Launch Plan

The fastest path is to tighten pages, tracking, and reply speed before broadening budget.

Days 1-7

Fix the offer surfaces

  • Pick the first two service lanes to advertise.
  • Tighten headline, proof, and CTA on each landing page.
  • Route all ad replies into support@mymultiplatform.com first.
Days 8-14

Launch the first campaigns

  • Build one Google Search campaign per local service lane.
  • Build one LinkedIn lead-gen offer for software / AI.
  • Use separate message angles instead of one generic company ad.
Days 15-21

Trim weak demand

  • Pause weak keywords, weak headlines, and weak audiences fast.
  • Keep only queries and audiences that create qualified replies.
  • Add proof blocks or FAQ content where friction appears.
Days 22-30

Scale what closes

  • Move budget toward the lane producing calls, site visits, or quotes.
  • Write one case-study style post from the best early lead.
  • Keep the losing lanes alive only if the message can still be improved.

Metrics That Matter

Early-stage ad performance should be judged by qualified conversations, not vanity reach.

Response time < 15 min

Reply speed is part of the funnel. A slow inbox makes paid traffic look worse than it is.

Qualified lead rate Track by lane

Measure how many leads are actually relevant for architecture, software, or security.

Booked next step Calls / quotes / visits

Count booked calls, quote requests, and site visits before thinking about scale.
