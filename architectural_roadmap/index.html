San Diego Relaunch Plan

Rebuild the business under MyMultiPlatform

This page is a restart roadmap for rebuilding the legacy company under MyMultiPlatform in San Diego. I am assuming the closest legacy scope is architectural millwork / cabinet / finish carpentry / shop drawings / installation because that is what the archive most strongly reflects. Research date: March 24, 2026.

Track A: millwork contractor Track B: licensed architecture practice Official sources only
Critical California naming warning The California Architects Board says that if a business uses the terms architect, architecture, or architectural in its name or service description to offer professional architectural services, the business must have a California licensed architect in management control of the professional services and in responsible control of all design services. CAB also says an LLC is not allowed to provide architectural services in California. If you are not yet operating with a California architect in control, the safer launch lane is the contractor / millwork business first, and the architecture practice second.

Recommended Structure

The cleanest rebuild is to separate what is clearly permitted today from what becomes permitted only after California architecture compliance is in place.

Phase 1

Launch the contractor / millwork company first

This is the closest match to the legacy operation shown by the archive. The primary CSLB lane is C-6 Cabinet, Millwork and Finish Carpentry, with optional future expansion into C-5 Framing and Rough Carpentry or B General Building if scope grows.

  • Use this lane for fabrication, finish carpentry, cabinetry, install, and millwork packages.
  • Build the operating company, insurance, estimating, and client pipeline here first.
  • Do not sell regulated architectural services through this entity unless CAB rules are satisfied.
Phase 2

Add the architecture practice only when compliant

If MyMultiPlatform will offer architectural services in California, the business needs a California architect in management control and responsible control, plus the correct CAB-compliant entity structure.

  • CAB says a general stock corporation, registered LLP, or professional corporation can be used for architectural services subject to restrictions.
  • CAB says a California LLC may not provide architectural services.
  • Architect license, BERF compliance, and written contracts become mandatory in this lane.
Practical recommendation

Use a two-lane migration

Rebuild the revenue engine under a contractor lane now, then formalize the architecture lane only when the licensing and entity structure are ready. That avoids the biggest regulatory mistake: selling an “architectural” service before CAB compliance is in place.

  • Lane 1: MyMultiPlatform millwork contractor.
  • Lane 2: MyMultiPlatform architecture practice, later.
  • Keep proposals and public copy aligned with the lane you are legally operating.

Licenses, Registrations, and Costs

This table is split between the contractor-first relaunch and the architecture-practice lane. Variable items are marked as such when the government source does not set the premium.

Item Lane When it is required Official cost Why it matters / note
California LLC formation Contractor lane only Use if the operating company is an LLC. $70 filing + $20 Statement of Information Good fit for a contractor or millwork company. CAB says an LLC is not allowed to provide architectural services in California.
California stock corporation formation Contractor or architecture lane Use if the operating company is a corporation. $100 filing + $25 Statement of Information Most flexible option if you want a contractor company now and may later align the entity for architecture work under CAB rules.
San Diego County Fictitious Business Name (DBA) Both lanes Required if you operate under a name different from the exact legal owner/entity name. $54 first name/registrant + $5 each additional County also requires publication for new filings; publication cost varies by newspaper and is not fixed by the county.
IRS EIN Both lanes Needed for an LLC, corporation, employees, banking, and most business registrations. $0 Get it directly from the IRS after the entity is formed.
City of San Diego Business Tax Certificate Both lanes Required for businesses operating in the City of San Diego. $38 base shown by the City, plus $1.47 per employee annually starting July 1, 2025 The city notes possible additional assessments for larger businesses or special districts.
CSLB original contractor application Contractor lane Required to obtain the California contractor license. $450 Use the contractor lane for millwork, cabinetry, finish carpentry, and installation work.
CSLB initial license fee Contractor lane Required before CSLB issues the license. $200 sole owner / $350 non-sole owner LLC or corporation will usually fall into the non-sole-owner bucket.
CSLB qualifier experience Contractor lane Required before approval. No filing fee beyond the application The qualifier must document at least four years of journeyman-level or higher experience in the classification sought, obtained within the last 10 years.
CSLB Live Scan background check Contractor lane Required with the contractor license process. $32 DOJ + $17 FBI + rolling fee varies by site The CSLB sets the state and federal processing fees; the Live Scan operator sets the rolling fee.
CSLB contractor bond Contractor lane Required before an active contractor license is issued. $25,000 bond amount required; premium varies CSLB requires the bond amount, but the actual premium is set by the surety/insurer, not by the state.
Bond of Qualifying Individual Contractor lane Required if the qualifier does not meet an ownership-based exemption. $25,000 bond amount if applicable; premium varies CSLB notes this can be waived in some ownership situations, especially where the RMO owns at least 10%.
Workers' compensation insurance Contractor lane Required if the business has one or more employees. Variable; not state-set DIR says all California employers must provide coverage. Premiums vary by carrier, payroll, and classification.
California seller's permit Contractor lane Needed if the business sells taxable fabricated goods, materials, or other taxable items. $0 CDTFA says there is no charge, but it may ask for a security deposit depending on the business.
EDD employer payroll tax account Contractor or architecture lane with employees Required if you hire employees. No registration fee listed by EDD This is the payroll tax account, not the payroll taxes themselves.
CSLB classification target Contractor lane Pick the classification that matches the real work. Included with the original application for the first class Closest legacy fit: C-6 Cabinet, Millwork and Finish Carpentry. Expansion options: C-5 Framing and Rough Carpentry and B General Building.
CAB eligibility evaluation Architecture lane Required for candidates who do not already hold a license in another jurisdiction. $100 This is part of the California architect licensure path.
California Supplemental Examination (CSE) Architecture lane Required for California architect licensure. $100 CAB also requires the ARE and the licensure experience path; those external exam costs are not included in this page.
CAB application for licensure Architecture lane Required before CAB issues the architect license. $400 CAB also requires ARE completion, AXP/IAP, and eight years of combined architectural training and education experience.
Architect's Business Entity Report Form (BERF) Architecture lane Required when a California architect provides architectural services through a business entity. No fee listed by CAB CAB says the board must be notified immediately of changes and an updated BERF must be filed within 30 days.
Written contract for architectural services Architecture lane Required before architectural work starts, unless the client authorizes earlier start in writing. No state filing fee CAB says architects must use written contracts when contracting to provide architectural services in California.
CAB renewal Architecture lane Required to keep the architect license active. $400 every two years Expired architects cannot legally practice architecture or use the title while expired.

This roadmap focuses on licenses and registrations. It does not include normal operating overhead such as rent, vehicles, payroll, fabrication equipment, accounting, franchise/income tax, general liability insurance, or external NCARB/ARE fees.

Scenario C

Add the architecture lane later

Add at least $600 in CAB fees

That adds CAB eligibility evaluation, the California Supplemental Examination, and the CAB licensure application. It does not include ARE / NCARB charges, nor any legal/accounting cost of forming the correct architecture entity.

  • Use this only when the architect license path is real and the entity is CAB-compliant.
  • CAB says an LLC may not provide architectural services in California.

30-Day Action Sequence

If the goal is to rebuild fast and legally, this is the order with the least regulatory friction.

  1. Choose the launch lane: contractor / millwork first unless the architecture lane is already compliant.
  2. Choose the entity: corporation if you want maximum future flexibility; LLC if this will stay a contractor-only company.
  3. File the California entity, then get the EIN.
  4. File the San Diego FBN only if the public-facing business name differs from the exact legal entity name.
  5. Register for the City of San Diego Business Tax Certificate.
  6. Pick the CSLB qualifier and collect the work experience proof for the C-6 application.
  7. Submit the CSLB original application, fingerprints, and then satisfy the bond / issuance items.
  8. Open the seller's permit if taxable fabricated goods or material sales will run through the company.
  9. Open the EDD payroll account and workers' comp only when hiring starts.
  10. If architecture services will be offered later, create the CAB-compliant entity and complete the California architect licensing / BERF / contract rules before public launch of that lane.
